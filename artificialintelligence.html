What is Artificial Intelligence ?

Artificial intelligence (or AI in short) is intelligence demonstrated by machines and this is the reason it is also regarded as “machine intelligence”. This artificial intelligence is not similar to the natural intelligence shown by human beings and animals. In the field of computer science, “AI Research” means the study and design of “intelligent agents”, where that “intelligent agent” can be any device which can take actions to successfully achieve a particular goal while remarking its environment at the same time.

LEARN MORE

Normally, the term AI can be applied when a machine imitates “intellectual” purposes like solving a problem (using past experiences) or learning a completely new thing. Artificial intelligence was instituted as an academic subject in 1956, and after that it have experienced numerous waves of optimism, which was then followed by disappointment and then loss of funding, followed by different approach, success and renewed funding. The research of Artificial Intelligence has been divided into sub-fields that are based on technical consideration like the goal or objective of that filed (like machine learning or robotics), these subfields often fails to communicate with each other. 

TRADITIONAL GOALS OF AI:

  1. Reasoning or Decision making – using past experiences or practices to take decisions and acknowledged situations.
  2. Knowledge representation – this field is dedicated to representing information about the environment that can be used by the machine to solve a problem.
  3. Learning – observing and learning new things. Get to know about the surroundings and be able to respond to that.
  1. Planning – planning includes choosing a series of tasks and actions to complete a specific goal.
  2. Natural Language Processing – this field is concerned with interaction between machines and the language used by humans. The main objective is to make the machine able to understand human language as it is spoken naturally.
  3. Perception – this involves enabling machine to collect sensory data and then interpret, reason and act according to that input.
Among all these, we have General Intelligence which is considered as the field’s long-term goal and uses all of the above listed fields and many more.
There are a large variety of tools used in AI, including kinds of search and mathematical optimization, neural networks, and procedures based on statistics and probability.
This field of Artificial intelligence draws upon computer science, mathematics, psychology, and many others.



Types of artificial intelligence :

ANI – Artificial Narrow Intelligence – It comprises of basic/role tasks such as those performed by chatbots, personal assistants like SIRI by Apple and Alexa by Amazon.
AGI – Artificial General Intelligence – Artificial General Intelligence comprises of human-level tasks such as performed by self-driving cars by Uber, Autopilot by Tesla. It involves continual learning by the machines.
ASI – Artificial Super Intelligence – Artificial Super Intelligence refers to intelligence way smarter than humans.

Future of AI :

The scope of AI is disputed: as machines become increasingly capable, tasks considered as requiring "intelligence" are often removed from the definition, a phenomenon known as the AI effect, leading to the quip, "AI is whatever hasn't been done yet." For instance, optical character recognition is frequently excluded from "artificial intelligence", having become a routine technology. Capabilities generally classified as AI as of 2017 include successfully understanding human speech, competing at the highest level in strategic game systems (such as chess and Go), autonomous cars, intelligent routing in content delivery network and military simulations.
